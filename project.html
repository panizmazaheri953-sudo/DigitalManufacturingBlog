Week 1 — Design Input

Brief Analysis
& Fusion 360
CAD Design

The project kicked off with a clear challenge: design two phone casings for Phones-R-Us. The bottom casing came with a complete engineering drawing — straightforward translation into Fusion 360. The top casing had no drawing — just a physical sample. That meant reverse engineering: callipers, cross-referencing views, deriving missing dimensions mathematically.

The very first decision was also one of the biggest: FDM 3D printing vs injection moulding. Injection moulding would cost £15,000–£40,000 in tooling alone before a single part could be made. FDM requires zero tooling — ideal for a 9-week sprint and a scalable annual contract.

Week 2 — Risk Analysis

PFMEA
12 Failure
Modes Mapped

Before a single print was attempted, every way the process could go wrong was mapped using a Process Failure Mode & Effects Analysis. 12 failure modes were identified across the print and assembly pipeline — from bed adhesion failures to sharp edges injuring operators.

The highest-risk failure was sharp edges on freshly printed parts — RPN of 400. This was immediately mitigated by adding chamfers to the CAD model and a mandatory deburring step in the process flow, bringing the RPN down to 80.

Weeks 3–4 — Print Trials (Phase 1)

Ultimaker
Trials.
All Failed.

The first print platform tested was the Ultimaker. Multiple prints were run, tweaking settings each time. Every single one failed at FESTO Station 2 — the laser measurement station. The laser consistently measured a wall depth of 1.18mm against a specification of 1.6–2.2mm.

This wasn't a calibration issue — it was a fundamental limitation of the Ultimaker's open-frame design causing inconsistent extrusion and warping. The data was clear: 0% pass rate across all Ultimaker prints at the critical dimension check. A platform pivot was inevitable.

Weeks 4–5 — Platform Switch

Bambu P1S
Pivot.
Problem Solved.

The switch to the Bambu Lab P1S was immediate. Its fully enclosed chamber eliminates the temperature gradients that caused warping on the Ultimaker. Print speed of up to 500mm/s meant faster iteration.

First print: 1.9mm wall thickness — right in the middle of the 1.6–2.2mm spec. FESTO Station 2 passed first time. Settings were locked, documented, and saved as the production profile. The pivot decision was validated by data within 48 hours.

Weeks 5–6 — Manufacturing Systems Design

MSD
Factory
Layout.

With the Bambu P1S confirmed as the production platform, the focus shifted to designing the full manufacturing system. Manufacturing Systems Design (MSD) covered the physical layout of the production cell, material flow from filament storage through to assembly despatch, and the configuration of the 25-printer fleet.

Key design decisions included printer arrangement for efficient operator access, filament spool management for 24/7 operation, and integration points with the FESTO CP Lab assembly line at the end of the production flow.

Weeks 6–7 — Digital Twin & Simulation

Tecnomatix
Plant Sim.
50K Proven.

A full Discrete Event Simulation was built in Siemens Tecnomatix Plant Simulation to validate whether 25 Bambu P1S printers could actually hit 50,000 casings per year. The digital twin modelled every print cycle, maintenance window, and failure event.

The capacity calculation confirmed the schedule: weekdays 24/7 and weekends 8h/day. This gives 150,790 usable print hours/year — enough for 60,316 attempts at 80% success = 48,253 good pairs, close enough to 50K when supplemented with minor overtime buffer.

Week 7 — Industry 4.0 & Safety

IoT &
Digital
Safety Net.

Running 25 printers overnight with no staff on-site is only viable with a robust Industrial IoT infrastructure. Siemens Insights Hub was configured for real-time telemetry from every printer — temperature, print progress, failure detection, and energy usage.

Beyond monitoring, the system includes physical safety measures: fire detection integrated with printer auto-shutoff, security alarms, and remote access control. If a printer fails at 3am, the system logs it, stops adjacent machines if needed, and alerts the engineer — no human needs to be present.

Week 8 — Business Model & Quality Lock

BMC &
SPC
Quality Lock.

With the production system proven, the Business Model Canvas (Lima & Baudier, 2017) was completed with real, verified numbers. The pricing of £5.50 per casing pair was calculated from actual costs: £470 Bambu P1S printers, £20/kg PLA filament, and a 20% failure rate factored in.

Simultaneously, Statistical Process Control was run on all dimensional measurements. Bambu P1S confirmed within spec at ±0.1mm. The Ultimaker was formally retired. Production parameters were locked — nothing changes without a documented change request from this point.
