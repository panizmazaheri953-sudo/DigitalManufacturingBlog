Visual Quality Control
Operator inspection · ~5 min per batch · 80% pass rate · 20% rework/reject
M
Inspect
Warping Check
~1min
Casing placed flat on reference surface. Corner lift > 0.5mm = reject. Warping caused by poor bed adhesion, draught, or incorrect bed temp. Most common failure mode.
M
Inspect
Surface Quality Check
~1min
Check for stringing, layer separation, holes, blobs, or under-extrusion. Any visible defect on the outer shell face = reject. Inner face allowed minor imperfections if functional.
M

~1min
Calipers used for spot-checks: wall thickness must be 1.6–2.2mm (target 1.9mm for FESTO S2 laser pass). 1-in-10 parts fully checked; remainder visual-only.
M

~1min
Front and back hand-pressed together to check clip engagement. Over-tight = elephant foot issue. Too loose = undersized print. Either = reject.

03 — Capacity
CAP

Production
Analysis.

Phones / Year
Capacity Analysis — 50,000 phones/year · 2.5h cycle time · 80% yield · Bambu P1S
📐 Where the 25 printers come from (full calculation):

1. Annual good pairs needed = 50,000
2. Required prints (80% yield) = 50,000 ÷ 0.8 = 62,500 pairs
3. Print cycle per pair = 2.5 hours → total print hours needed = 62,500 × 2.5 = 156,250 hours

4. Operating hours per printer per year:
  Weekdays: 261 days × 24h = 6,264 hours
  Weekends: 104 days × 8h = 832 hours
  Total = 7,096 hours/year per printer

5. Theoretical printers needed = 156,250 ÷ 7,096 = 22.0 printers
6. Adding 15% buffer (maintenance, variation) = 22 × 1.15 = 25.3 → 25 printers ✅
Operating Scenario Hours / Day Calculation Printers Required Annual Output
8-Hour Shift (9–5, single shift) 8h 600 ÷ 8 hrs 75 printers 50,000 phones
16-Hour Shift (two shifts) 16h 600 ÷ 16 hrs 38 printers 50,000 phones
★ 24/7 Operation (Recommended) 24h 600 ÷ 24 hrs 25 printers 50,000 phones
24/7 + 20% Maintenance Buffer 24h 25 × 1.2 30 printers 60,000 phones (buffer)
→ Recommendation:
25 Bambu P1S printers running 24/7 is the optimal configuration to meet the 50,000 phones/year target. This is equivalent to operating 75 printers on a single 8-hour shift leading a capital saving of £50,000+ in printer hardware alone, but requires a robust remote monitoring infrastructure to manage unattended overnight operation (addressed via camera system and cloud dashboard).

⚙️ How the FESTO Line Actually Works — Pipelined Flow
Wrong assumption: One case enters S1, waits for all 6 stations to finish (99s), then the next case enters.

Reality: The FESTO line is a pipeline — a new case enters S1 every 16.5 seconds (99s ÷ 6 stations). While Case A is at S6, Case B is at S5, Case C at S4, and so on. This means a completed case exits S6 every 16.5 seconds, not every 99 seconds. The total line throughput is ~218 cases per hour, far above the 150s takt requirement.

Formula: Throughput time per case = Total cycle time ÷ Number of stations = 99s ÷ 6 = 16.5s per case

Why 2 per shift? Each operator can realistically manage 12–13 printers, collecting prints every 2.5h, cleaning beds, changing filament, starting new jobs, and visual QC. 25 printers requires 2 operators. IoT alarms and Bambu Cloud handle monitoring; engineers are alerted if anything fails overnight.
Operator Tasks Per Shift
Print collection — collect completed pairs from print bed every 2.5h (~3 batches per 8h shift)
Bed cleaning — clean and re-prepare build plate between runs to prevent adhesion failures
Filament management — monitor spool levels, change PLA rolls before depletion mid-print
Job loading — start next print job via Bambu Cloud; verify settings match locked production profile
Visual QC — inspect each print for warping, surface defects, incomplete layers
FESTO S3 iPick — manually place and press casings together at Station 3
Reject handling — log failed prints (20% rate), clear bed, restart

Formula according to AIAG and VDA (2019)
Cp = (USL − LSL) ÷ (6 × σ)
Cpk = min[(USL − μ) ÷ (3σ), (μ − LSL) ÷ (3σ)]

Cp = 2.0 (Excellent. Process spread fits within specification.) Cpk = 1.87 (Well centred and capable.)
Cp = (2.2 − 1.6) ÷ (6 × 0.05) = 0.6 ÷ 0.3 = 2.0. The process spread is only 50 percent of the specification width.
With mean μ = 1.91mm, Cpk = min[(2.2 − 1.91) ÷ 0.15, (1.91 − 1.6) ÷ 0.15] = min[1.93, 2.07] = 1.87. The process is well centred with no risk of exceeding limits.
Both Cp and Cpk are above 1.33, confirming the Bambu P1S fleet is statistically capable for the critical wall thickness dimension (Montgomery, 2019).
📏 Measurement System Variation
Six different operators measuring the same casing gave a measurement range of 0.12mm.
One trained operator performing all quality control measurements reduced variation to 0.04mm.
Decision: All wall thickness checks on the FESTO S2 laser station are performed by the same trained operator where possible. Critical measurements are not sacrosanct facts. They are levels of agreement between humans (Lecture 8).

⚠ Bottleneck Recommendation
Station 5 (Oven) at 35s is the FESTO line bottleneck. However, as the total FESTO cycle (99s) remains well within the 150s takt, the bottleneck does not currently limit production. If demand scales beyond 50,000 pairs/year, a second FESTO oven module or parallel FESTO line would be the recommended investment.
