02 — The Decision
WHY

Why We
Switched.

From

⚡ Platform Switch — Data-Driven
20 prints. 0 passed FESTO Station 2. Wall consistently at 1.18mm vs 1.6–2.2mm spec. The data was unambiguous — the platform had to change.
To
Bambu P1S
Why Ultimaker Failed
Root Cause 1
Open frame = temperature gradients. Without an enclosure, ambient air cooled different parts of the print unevenly, causing layer warping and inconsistent extrusion — directly resulting in the 1.18mm wall measurement.
Root Cause 2
Bowden tube extruder. The longer filament path between motor and nozzle reduces retraction precision and introduces pressure lag, causing inconsistent extrusion particularly in thin-wall sections.
Root Cause 3
Hardware reliability. Multiple mid-print failures due to filament sensor errors, nozzle clogs, and calibration drift across ~20 attempts. Unacceptable for a 50,000/year production commitment.
Why Bambu P1S Solved It
Solution 1
Fully enclosed chamber. Eliminates temperature gradients entirely. The entire print environment is thermally stable, preventing the differential cooling that caused warping on the Ultimaker.
Solution 2
Direct drive extruder. Filament motor sits directly above the nozzle — precise retraction, consistent extrusion pressure, and reliable thin-wall printing. Achieved 1.9mm wall first attempt.
Solution 3
500mm/s speed + reliability. Faster print cycle (2.5h for both casings), cloud monitoring, and auto-calibration dramatically improve hardware stability and enable 24/7 unattended operation.

Failure 01 — Ultimaker

Across approximately 20 Ultimaker attempts, multiple prints stopped mid-way through. Causes included filament sensor triggers, layer adhesion failures causing the part to detach from the bed mid-print, and calibration drift between print jobs. Each failure meant a wasted 1–2 hours and material loss.
Why this kept happening
The Ultimaker's open-frame design means any vibration, temperature change, or filament inconsistency directly affects the print. No auto-calibration, no closed-loop feedback — operator had to manually re-level and restart every time.
Failure 02 — Ultimaker
Nozzle
Clogging.
Blocked nozzle
Under-extrusion on print
Failed casing — nozzle clog
Nozzle clogs occurred repeatedly across the Ultimaker trials. A partial blockage causes under-extrusion — the printer attempts to deposit filament but the reduced flow rate means walls are thinner than specified. This directly contributed to the 1.18mm wall measurement. Full clogs stopped prints entirely.
Root cause
The Ultimaker's Bowden tube extruder creates pressure inconsistencies in the filament path. Any small debris or heat creep in the tube can build pressure until the nozzle partially blocks. Required manual cold-pull cleaning and recalibration between affected prints.
Failure 03 — Ultimaker
Layer Change
Defects.
Layer shift — visible offset
Delamination between layers
Visible gap in wall
Several Ultimaker prints showed clear layer shifting — where the upper portion of the print is offset horizontally from the lower portion, creating a visible step in the casing wall. Separately, poor inter-layer adhesion caused delamination, where layers separated under light mechanical force.
Why this happens on the Ultimaker
Layer shifts occur when the stepper motor skips steps — typically from excessive print speed, belt tension issues, or vibration. The open frame amplifies vibration. Poor layer adhesion results from layers cooling too fast in the open-air environment before the next layer bonds properly.

FESTO Station 2 — PASS ✓
This was the decisive failure. Several Ultimaker prints looked visually acceptable — smooth surface, correct shape. But every single one failed at FESTO Station 2 (laser measurement). The wall was consistently 1.18mm — below the 1.6mm minimum specification. The part looked right but measured wrong.
Why this is a critical failure, not a cosmetic one
The wall thickness specification is not aesthetic — it determines structural rigidity, press-fit force at Station 4, and long-term durability in a phone assembly. A 1.18mm wall is 26% below minimum specification. No amount of post-processing could fix this — it required a new print on a better machine.
Failure 05 — Ultimaker
Visual
Failures.
Warped bottom casing
Surface defects
Rejected casing
A significant proportion of Ultimaker prints failed at visual inspection before even reaching FESTO — warped edges, delaminated surface layers, stringing between features, and incomplete sections from mid-print failures. These were rejected immediately and the prints restarted.
Underlying cause
Warping is caused by differential thermal contraction — the bottom of the print cools and shrinks while the top is still being deposited at high temperature. The open-frame Ultimaker has no way to prevent this. The Bambu's enclosed chamber keeps the entire part at a consistent elevated temperature throughout the print.
