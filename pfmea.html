05 — Insight
↑
Engineering Conclusions

Student
Reflection.

01 — Printer as Control Point
Equipment selection is a process variable

Traditional PFMEA treats process capability as fixed — this project showed that printer selection is itself a controllable variable. The Ultimaker consistently failed (RPN 288) while the Bambu P1S passed (RPN 64). Switching printers was the corrective action, not redesigning the CAD.

02 — Economic Analysis
24/7 vs shift-based operation

75 printers on an 8-hour shift produces the same as 25 printers running 24/7 — a hardware capital saving of over £50,000. However, 24/7 introduces unattended operation risk (PFMEA 40.01, RPN 144). The remote monitoring system was the enabling corrective action that made 24/7 viable.

03 — CAD Was Never the Problem
Design validated through process failure

All 15 Ultimaker prints failed measurement — yet when the same CAD file was printed on the Bambu P1S, 80% passed first time. This confirms the dimensional specification was correct and achievable. PFMEA prevented the team from incorrectly blaming the design file.

04 — Safety First Priority
RPN 400 — highest risk addressed first

The highest RPN in the entire analysis wasn't a dimensional issue — it was operator safety (RPN 400, SEV 10). Sharp casing edges identified as a hazard drove an immediate design change to chamfered edges and a mandatory deburring station, reducing RPN to 80.

64

Conclusion: Switching to Bambu P1S reduced the occurrence score from 9 to 2, cutting the primary print RPN from 288 → 64. For 50,000 units/year, 25 Bambu P1S printers running 24/7 — supported by cloud monitoring, remote camera systems, and fireball suppression — is the recommended production configuration. All safety-class items have been actioned and verified.
