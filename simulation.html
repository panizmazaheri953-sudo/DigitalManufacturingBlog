01 — The Method
DES

What Is
Plant Simulation?

Discrete Event Simulation (DES)
A method of modelling a production system where each event is processed in sequence — parts move through stations, machines start and stop, queues build up. Tecnomatix simulates thousands of production cycles in seconds, revealing bottlenecks and capacity limits before any physical investment is made.
Siemens Tecnomatix Plant Simulation
Industry-standard simulation software used by manufacturers worldwide to validate production capacity, optimise layouts, and identify bottlenecks digitally. Used here to confirm the 25-printer Bambu P1S fleet can deliver 50,000 casing pairs per year within the defined schedule.
Why Simulation Before Production?
Buying 25 Bambu P1S printers (£11,750 capital) without first validating they can hit 50,000 units/year would be a significant financial risk. DES removes that uncertainty — the simulation proves the fleet size, schedule, and layout before any physical commitment is made.
Build-Then-Validate Approach
Rather than modelling everything at once, the simulation was built incrementally — FESTO CP Lab first, then robot material handling, then the full printer fleet. Each stage was validated before the next was added, ensuring the model was accurate at every layer.
Why Tecnomatix specifically?
Tecnomatix Plant Simulation is part of the Siemens Digital Industries Software suite — the same ecosystem used by automotive, aerospace, and electronics manufacturers globally. It integrates directly with IoT platforms like Siemens Insights Hub, making it the natural choice for a project that includes real-time monitoring and digital twin functionality. The simulation outputs can be directly connected to live production data, turning a planning tool into an ongoing operational asset.

04 — Findings
OUT

What the Simulation
Proved.

50K
Units/year — confirmed
The 25-printer fleet on the weekday 24/7 + weekend 8h schedule delivers approximately 48,250–50,000 pairs/year — meeting the Phones-R-Us contract commitment with a small overtime buffer.
25
Optimal printer fleet size
Simulation confirmed 25 is the right number. Fewer printers miss the target. More printers add capital cost without proportional output gain. 25 × £470 = £11,750 — justified by simulation.
S5
Bottleneck — FESTO Oven (35s)
Station 5 (Oven) at 35s is the FESTO line constraint. Currently within the 150s takt — no action needed. If demand exceeds 50K/year, a second oven module is the recommended investment.
99s
FESTO total cycle — within takt
The complete FESTO 6-station cycle of 99 seconds comfortably fits within the 150-second takt time, meaning the assembly line is not the production constraint — the printer fleet is the pacing element.
✓
80% yield absorbed in capacity
The 20% print failure rate was built into the simulation from day one. The system still hits 50K because 62,500 print attempts/year produce 50,000 good pairs — the failure rate is planned for, not an exception.
3×
Capital saved vs single shift
A single-shift scenario would require 75 printers to hit 50K/year — three times the fleet and capital cost. Running 25 printers 24/7 is the financially optimal configuration, confirmed by simulation.
Connected System
This simulation feeds directly into the IoT & Digital Twin architecture
The Tecnomatix model is not a one-off planning exercise — it forms the baseline digital twin of the production system. Live data from Siemens Insights Hub and Bambu Cloud can be fed back into the simulation to update it as real production data accumulates, creating a continuously improving model of the factory.
